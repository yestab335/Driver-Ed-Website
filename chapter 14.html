Multiple Choice
  1. The three categories of distractions include visual, manual, and ___.
    1. Synergistic
    2. Mental
    3. Complex
    4. Compound
  2. How can being distracted affect the core skills needed to drive safely?
    1. Slowed perceptions
    2. Delayed decision-making
    3. Improper action
    4. All of the above
  3. After using voice commands to make a call, send a text, or change the music, mental distractions can last up to ___ seconds.
    1. 2
    2. 12
    3. 18
    4. 27
  4. The most effective way to prevent becoming distracted by a cell phone while driving is:
    1. Put your cell phone on silent.
    2. Do not engage in emotional conversations while driving.
    3. Turn on your phone's Do Not Disturb mode.
    4. Keep your conversations short.
  5. If you encounter a suspected distracted driver, your first step should be to:
    1. Put more space between you and that driver.
    2. Safely come to a stop.
    3. Obtain information about the vehicle-type, make, model, and license plate number.
    4. Call 9-1-1.

Short Answer
  1. Explain three ways being distracted can increase risk. Three ways being distracted can increase risk is by lowering alertness, lowering focus on the road and other vehicles, and increases the chance of death during a car accident.
  2. Explain why the risk of crashing rises as the number of passengers under age 21 increases. The risk of crashing rises as the number of passengers under age 21 increases because there are more distractions for the adolescent mind.
  3. Explain the difference in crash risk between using a cell phone in handheld mode versus hands-free mode. Both using a cell phone in handheld or in hands-free mode are equally dangerous.
  4. List three techniques to prevent distractions before starting to drive. Three techniques to prevent distractions before starting to drive is to check your car and mirrors, place your phone on Do Not Disturb mode, and settle all distractions before shifting out of park.
  5. List three techniques to manage distractions as they occur while driving. Three techniques to manage distractions as they occur while driving is to calm down and get a safe distance between you and the suspected driver. Lower the volume of the radio, and place your phone on Do Not Disturb mode.
