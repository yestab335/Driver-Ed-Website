Short Answer
  1. List the pre-driving checks involved in safely approaching your vehicle. The pre-driving checks include, keeping you keys in your hand, be aware of other pedestrians and drivers, look for signs and movement between, underneath, and around objects on both sides of the vehicle, double-check that other vehicles can see you, and if parked at the curb, face traffic as you approach the driver's door.
  2. Explain how safety restraints can help protect vehicle occupants in a collision. Safety restraints can help protect vehicle occupants in a collision in many ways. Some of them being is safety restraints can slow the occupants' rate of deceleration in a frontal collision, and keep occupants securely in place during any random directional change.
  3. Describe the optimal driver seating position. The optimal driver seating positionis to be seated in a comfortable, upright position, with a slight bend in your arms when grabbing the steering wheel.
  4. Explain how to set vehicle side-view mirrors, and how they help drivers monitor the space around their vehicles. In order to properly set vehicle's left side-view mirror, the driver will have to rest their head against the window and adjust the mirror to the point where the right corner reveals the rear of the vehicle. As for the right side-view mirror, the driver will have to lean over the center console to the point where the mirror is slightly above their head and adjust the same way they did for the left side-view mirror. Side-view mirror help drivers moniter space around their vehicle by revealing and opening up some blind spots that come with driving.
  5. Describe eight items that can appear on an instrument panel. Eight items that can appear on an instrument panel are; fuel guage, location of fuel door, tachometer, battery warning light, Parking brake indicator, left-turn indicator, right-turn indicator, and speedometer.
