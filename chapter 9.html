Short Answer
  1. Outline the general procedure for safely changing lanes. Check your mirrors, make sure you have space to the front, back, or sides. Turn on your turn signal. Double check you still have space, then proceed to change lanes.
  2. List three common committed errors that new drivers tend to make when chenging lanes. Three common committed errors that new drivers tend to make when changing lanes are no use of their blinker, no change in speed, and getting too close to the car in the new lane.
  3. Outline the proper procedure for reversing direction by backing into a right-side driveway. Check behind you and the driveway, tap your brake lights to signal to drivers behind you, shift into reverse and if it is clear begin turning the wheel, and back only until the vehicle is stright.
  4. Outline the procedures for entering and exiting a perpendicular parking space. When entering a perpendicular parking space, you need to monitor vehicles to make sure no one's backing out, use break lights and turn signals, position 8-9 feet from the rear of the vehicle that is parked, steer wheel rapidly to enter pace and move forward slowly. When exiting a perpendicular parking space, you need to check for obstructions, signal, reverse while looking back, slowly back straight out, and slowly turn toward the path of travel.
  5. Explain how to use a reference point to determine how far to pull forward as you enter a perpendicular parking space. You can determine where the front of your vehicle is by projecting a sight line just under the driver and passenger side view mirror.
