Short Answer
  1. Describe three ways in ehich drivers and vehicle owners can demonstrate proof of financial responsibility. One-way drivers and vehicle owners can demonstrate proof of financial responsibility is by possessing automobile liability-incurance coverage. Another way is by depositing money in the amount required by the state. And a third way is to deposit bonds or other securities (title to preperty or stock certicicate) sufficient to meet the state's requirements.
  2. List four principles of right-of-way. The purpose is to prevent conflicts resulting from one driver failed to yield to another. To be granted the right-of-way, driver must be driving in a lawful manner. In other words, a driver cannot take the right-of-way by breaking another law. A driver has not yielded the right-of-way if they force other drivers to slow or wait. Whoever has the last chance to avoid a collision, has the obligation to do so.
  3. How should drivers respond to an approaching emergency vehicle using its lights and siren? When an emergency vehicle approaches using their lights and sirens, all other drivers should drive to a position parallel to and close as possible to the right-hand edge of the road and stop and remain there until the emergency vehicle has passed by. Making sure to stay clear of any intersection.
  4. Explain the difference(s) between aggressive driving and road rage. Aggressive driving is generally defined as behaviors indicative of an aggressive mind set; not necessarily directed to another driver. This includes speeding, running red lights, changing lanes without using the turn signals, and following too closely or tailgating. Road Rage is generally defined as behaviors directed at specific vehicles or occupants with malicious intent.
  5. Describe the conditions under which a driver can be charged with driving under the influence of alcohol or other drugs. When a driver is pulled over and their BAC test result is either at or above 0.08%, they can be charged with a DWI or DUI. Courts generally accept tests on blood, breath, or urine to determine the BAC of a driver. Drivers could also be charged at any BAC level if they're found to exhibit loss of normal us of mental/physical abilities.
