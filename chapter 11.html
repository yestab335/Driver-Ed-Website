Chapter 11 Homework


Multiple Choice
  1. By design, intersections are risky because:
    1. Traffic signals might not always work.
    2. Drivers pay close attention to other road usres.
    3. Vehicles cross one another's paths of travel at various angles.
    4. Pedestrians typically cross roads and streets quickly.
  2. Intersection types include:
    1. Open and closed
    2. Warning and regulatory
    3. Line of sight and path of travel
    4. Controlled and uncontrolled
  3. Successfully navigating intersections involves:
    1. Identifying intersections well in advanced of reaching them
    2. Managing speed appropriately
    3. Adjusting positions as necessary
    4. All of the above
  4. An important difference between expressways and freeways is that expressways feature intersections, whereas freeways use:
    1. Interchanges
    2. Medians
    3. Rumble stripes
    4. Green guide signs
  5. Three main parts of freeway driving are:
    1. Signaling, turning, stopping
    2. Distraction, fatigue, emotion
    3. Getting on, driving on, getting off
    4. Shifting, backing, idling

Short Answer
  1. Explain how to lawfully and safely turn right at a red traffic signal. In order to lawfully and safely turn right at a red traffic signal a driver must approach slowly, yield for pedestrians, stop and turn when it's safe.
  2. Describe at least four advantages of roundabouts over traditional intersections. Four advantages of roundabouts over traditional intersections are that they're safe, faster, eco-friendly, and cheaper.
  3. Explain the steps to properly merge onto a freeway. Search for a safe gap, maintain same safe space around vehicles, signal and accelerate, and adjust your speed.
  4. Describe "velocitation" and "highway hypnosis." Becoming used to driving fast is velocitation and driving for long periods of time and going into hypnosis from looking at the road for so long is highway hypnosis.
  5. Define "weave lane" and explain how it is used. The weave lane is the lane on the very far side of the highway and can either be on the left or right side of the road. It is used for exiting and entering a freeway.
