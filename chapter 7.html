    1. Central
    2. Tunnel
    3. Fringe
    4. Peripheral

    1. Targeting scope
    2. Visual scope
    3. Targeting lead
    4. Visual lead
  3. Drivers should search at least ___ seconds ahead.
    1. 3 to 4
    2. 9 to 10
    3. 12 to 15
    4. 20 to 30
  4. Drivers should maintain a visual control zone of at least ___ seconds ahead.
    1. 3 to 4
    2. 9 to 10
    3. 12 to 15
    4. 20 to 30
  5. To effectively search the total traffic scene, drivers should monitor the ___ of the vehicle.
    1. To the rear
    2. To the sides
    3. To the front
    4. All of the above

Short Answer
  1. Define sentral vision. Central vision is a 5-degree cone that makes up only 3% of a driver's total visual field. It provides details of objects and conditions and is the primary visual function employed in targeting.
  2. Define fringe vision. Fringe vision is used to jidge depth and position. It provides information about objects close to the travel path and lane position.
  3. Define peripheral vision Peripheral vision is a conical in shape around the other vision field. It detects color and motion, providing you with information about moving or stationary objects that could be threats along the intended path of travel.
  4. Describe how to perform ground viewing and explain how this technique can reduce driving risk. Search the visual scene near the ground to help you detect important information to support your safe driving. This technique can reduce driving risk because the driver will be alert to other roadway users, pedestrians, roadway markings or signs and road conditions.
  5. Explain why drivers shoulld have their low-beam headlights on during daylight hours. Drivers should have their low-beam headlights on during daylight hours to make it easier to spot oncoming traffic and any other cars around you.
