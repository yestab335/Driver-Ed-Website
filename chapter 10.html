Short Answer
  1. Describe your next actions as a driver when you see several children who appear ready to run across a neighborhood street as you approach. Slow and let them pass and then drive when it is safe.
  2. List three strategies for reducing risk when sharing the road with emergency vehicles. Three strategies for reducing risk when sharing the road with emergency vehicles are to pull over, stop, and then drive when it is safe.
  3. Describe ways in which you should adjust your driving near large trucks. Give them a lot of room and be aware that they have many blind spots.
  4. How can drivers reduce risk at railroad crossings? Drivers can reduve risk at railroad crossings by stopping, standing fifteen feet clear, and then drive when it's safe.
  5. Explain what to do and how to behave as a driver stopped by the police. Keep your hands in plain view at all times, perferable on the steering wheel and refrain from making any sudden movements. Wait for the officer to request your license, registration, and evidence of insurance.
