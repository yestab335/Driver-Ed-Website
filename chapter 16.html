    1. Drive slower.

    3. Pull over to the side of the road.

Short Answer
  1. It is a very foggy day, and you experience highly reduced visibility. What can you do to be seen? Turn on low beam headlights, follow basic speed law, check if vehicle has fog light, driving lights, or a fog tail lamp that is operable and working, hazard flashers if the weather is severely foggy, and turn on defroster with air conditioner.
  2. State five rules to help reduce the challenges of night driving. Don't drive around bed time, 2 to 4 p.m., are "low" times, clean windshield, windows, and mirrors, make sure all headlights, tail lights, and license plate lights are working, and most importantly keep your brain and eyes active.
  3. List five tips for safely and successfully navigating temporary work zones. Stay alert, obey posted speed limits, minimize distractions, follow the direction of flaggers, and be patient.
  4. Identify three factors that can increase risk when driving in rural/country environments. Branches, animals, and two-way roads/poorly or unlit roads.
  5. List three risk associated with high-altitude mountain driving. Less oxygen, driving up steep hills, and the engine generates less power due to the high altitude.
