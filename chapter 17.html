Short Answer
  1. Describe the Basic Speed Law. The basic speed law indicates that a driver should be cautious to other vehicles and pedestrians. Drive at a cautious speed. And give other drivers optimum space for driving safely.
  2. Explain how to respond and recover if your vehicle runs off the road. One firm grip on the steering wheel, ease off the accelerator and coast down to guide the vehicle, gently apply pressure on the brake when it is safe to stop.
  3. List five vehicle failures and how you can avoid or overcome them. One type of failure is brake failure. This can be overcomed by making routine checks, pumping the brake pedal, and applying steady pressure. Another type of failure is light failure. It can be maintained by making routine checks before and after driving. Engine failures can occur by stalling, overheating, and failure to start. Brake failures can also occur. They can be overcame by pullnig over to the side of the road and letting the vehicle coast to a complete stop, using the emergency brake, shift between gears, and putting the vehilce in park. And a fifth vehicle failure that can occur is unintended acceleration. This can be overcame by appying brakes before shifting from park and shifting to neutral.
  4. Explain the "three-collision concept." Impact occurs between the vehicle and another object. Second collision, fraction of a second later, between the occupants and some part of the vehicle's interrior. And the third collision is the body's internal organs strike other body parts.
  5. List your responsibilities if you are driving a vehicle involved in a crash. Stop, assist the injured, control the scene, notify the police, and exchange required information (names, vehicle id number, registration forms, and insurance information.
