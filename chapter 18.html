Short Answer
  1. Describe three driving techniques for economical and enviromentally responsible driving. Have you car facing the parking lot so you can get out faster and safer. Before driving, carefully plan your route to include all of your destinations. This can help reduce the total number of trips. Consider carpooling, where you are going to share driving responsibilities with other people. The help reduce the number of vehicles on the road, tailpipe emissions and your personal fuel costs.
  2. Explain how to use a quarter to check tire tread depth. Put a quarter in the tread and if you can see the head you need to change the tires. But if the head is hidden you do not need to change the tires.
  3. Explain the effects of low tire pressure on fuel economy and exhaust emissions. Under inflation by 20% decreases fuel economy and has the same effect on a tire as a 30% increase in speed. While it may result in a somewhat firmer ride, inflating tires to the pressure recommended for long distance, high-speed travel will previde greater tread life.
  4. Describe the advantages of preperly maintaining a vehilce. Some advantages include having a more reliable vehicles, reduce harmful exhaust, and it will cost less to take care of.
  5. People who drive an average of 12,000 miles a year should occasionally check items that affect their vehicles' safety effciency. List as many of these items as you are able. Window cracks, scratchesm dirt, brakes, lights, tread thickness, tire alignment, leaks, dents, wipers, washer sluid level, suspension, leaning, and swaying.
