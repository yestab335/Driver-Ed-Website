Short Answer
  1. Identify five groups of drivers who are at increased risk of becoming drowsy behind the wheel. Five groups of drivers who are at increased risk of becoming drowsy behind the wheel are young drivers, drivers on the road after extended wakefulness, drivers on long trips, drivers with undiagnosed or untreated sleep disorders, and drivers on the road during the "low" times.
  2. Describe three pre-driving steps to help prevent drowsiness while driving. Three pre-driving steps to help prevent drowsiness while driving is to get a full night's sleep, driving with a passenger, and avoid driving after drug consumption.
  3. Name five ways in which a person's health and overall quality of life can be affected by lack of sleep. Hair loss, puffy dark eyes, tiredness, skin problems, and bad judgment.
  4. List six guidelines to help you avoid driving aggressively and engaging in road rage. Allow plenty of time to reach you destinations, be calm at all times, maintain enough space, do not block any lanes, always signal before turning or changing lanes, and use your horn only when necessary.
  5. Explain five strategies for effectively responding to a road user who is behaving aggressively toward you. Don't respond, avoid eye contact, remain calm and take deep breaths, be understanding and supportive, and be polite and courteous.
