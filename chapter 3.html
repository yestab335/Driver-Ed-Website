Short Answer
  1. Define hydroplaning, and explain how drivers can prevent hydroplaning from happening. Hydroplaning is when a vehicle slides uncontrollably on the wet surface of a road. One way a driver can prevent hydroplaning from happening is by driving slow when their vehicle is on a wet surface.
  2. List and explain factors that affect traction between a vehicle's tires and a road surface. The material the orad surface is made from, the condition of the road, the condition of the vehicle tires, and the balance of your vehicle are all factors that can affect a vehicle's tranction to any road surface.
  3. List four substances that can reduce traction between a vehicle's tires and a road surface. Four substances that can reduce traction between a vehicle's tires and a road surface are; asphalt, sand, dirt, gravel, and brick roads.
  4. Explain the relationship between inertia and traction. Inertia has an impact on traction. Simply put, until acted upon by some external force, an item in motion continues to travel straight forward. When a driver abruptly changes the direction of a vehicle, this force causes a problem.
  5. Natural laws affect vehicles traveling up and down hills. Explain how hills influence vehicle speed and the ability of drivers to accelerate and decelerate. Vehicle speed decreases as a vehicle goes upward and increases as a vehicle goes downward due to the gravitational pull. The ability to accelerate decreases as a vehicle does upward and increases as a vehicle does downward due to the gravitational pull. And the ability to decelerate increases as a vehicle does upward and decreases as a vehicle goes downward due to the gravitational pull.
